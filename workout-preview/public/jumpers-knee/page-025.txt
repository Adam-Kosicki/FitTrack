 
 
 
 
 
JUMPER’S KNEE PROTOCOL 
Store-And-Release progressions are done in a gradual manner to improve patellar tendon 
function without causing flare ups.  Each category progresses from low (e.g., 50% efforts) to 
high (e.g., 90-100% efforts) intensity and from high (e.g., 80 jumps, 600 yards) to low (e.g., 20 
jumps, 150 yards) volume.  Outside of this, there are other things done to slowly increase 
patellar tendon stress (noted in parenthesis), they are listed here: 
 
1) LANDING PROGRESSION 
 
- 
Bilateral Land (very low) 
- 
Split Land (low) 
- 
Staggered Land (medium) 
- 
Unilateral Land (high) 
 
2) JUMPING PROGRESSION  
 
- 
Band-Assisted Jump (very low) 
- 
Seated Jump (low) 
- 
Standing Jump (medium) 
- 
Approach Jump (high) 
 
3) ACCELERATION/DECELERATION 
 
- 
Tempo Intervals (very low) 
- 
Flying Sprints (low) 
- 
Accel/Decel 70% (medium) 
- 
Accel/Decel 90-100% (high)  
 
 
4) CHANGE OF DIRECTION (COD) 
 
- 
45-degrees (low) 
- 
90-degrees (medium) 
- 
180-degrees small area (medium) 
- 
180-degrees big area (high)
