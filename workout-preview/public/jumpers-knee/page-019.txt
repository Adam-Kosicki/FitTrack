3) STEP UP  
 
Pick a step up variation that you tolerate 
well and can progressively add load to 
over time.  Try to keep the starting knee 
bend around 60-degrees.  Stand up and 
lower without letting the knee snap back 
or extend excessively.  Keep the knee 
soft through the set.  Same tempo, sets, 
reps, rest, as day 1 squats and split 
squats.

Front Step Up.

Side Step Up. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
4) LATERAL SLED DRAG 
 
Similar to the lateral lunge in day 1, this 
exercise will get some hip internal rotation and 
pronation of the foot.  Go a distance of about 
20 yards each side.  It shouldn’t be so heavy 
that you feel it difficult to internally rotate the 
hip and get a good pronation push with the 
foot.  Take smaller steps if needed and make 
them larger as your range opens up.  Rest as 
needed between sets.
