 
 
 
 
 
JUMPER’S KNEE PROTOCOL 
WHEN TO PROGRESS 
 
BENCHMARKS TO ENTER STAGE 3 STORE-AND-RELEASE: 
 
• Next day pain is regularly at a 3/10 or less, using either: 
➢ How you feel in the morning 
➢ Pain-provocation test 
• Improvements in Quadriceps, Calves, and Entire Leg function: 
➢ Your KNEE-DOMINANT SQUAT and HIP HINGE numbers have noticeably improved 
(research recommends at least 1.5 times bodyweight for an estimated squat max). 
➢ Your SPLIT SQUAT and STEP UP have also improved and are consistent side-to-side. 
➢ You can perform the LATERAL LUNGE and LATERAL SLED DRAG without much restriction 
in range of motion (e.g., hips internally rotate, and feet pronate well). 
• Elite-level Hopping ability: 
➢ You have completed the ACHILLES HOPPING PROGRESSION and can perform single-leg 
tuck jumps with good ankle stiffness and confidence. 
You will still use this same lifting workout for the subsequent stages.  For the knee-dominant 
squat and hip hinge, continue to get stronger.  Same for the split squat, step up, soleus, and 
gastroc.  For the lateral lunge and lateral sled drag, continue to make improvements in hip 
internal rotation and foot pronation.  For the achilles hopping progression (once you hit the last 
progression), start it over at the first exercise and run through it again in the same sequence.
